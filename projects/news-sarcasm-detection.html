News Headline Sarcasm Detection

Python NLP PyTorch Transformers BERT

An NLP system for detecting sarcasm in news headlines using advanced machine learning techniques. Combines transformer models with feature engineering to achieve high accuracy in sarcasm classification, helping readers better understand the tone and intent of news content.

Problem

Sarcasm in text is notoriously difficult to detect, especially in short headlines where context is limited. Traditional sentiment analysis models often misclassify sarcastic content, leading to incorrect interpretation of news tone and intent.

The challenge involves creating a model that can accurately identify sarcasm in news headlines, which requires understanding subtle linguistic cues, context, and cultural references.

Approach

Developed a multi-stage classification pipeline combining transformer models with traditional NLP features. The approach includes:

  • Transformer Backbone: Fine-tuned BERT and RoBERTa models on sarcasm datasets
  • Feature Engineering: Lexical, syntactic, and semantic features
  • Ensemble Methods: Combining multiple models for improved accuracy
  • Data Augmentation: Techniques to handle class imbalance

Results

The system achieved strong performance on sarcasm detection:

  • Accuracy: 87% F1-score on test dataset
  • Precision: 89% precision for sarcastic headlines
  • Recall: 85% recall, capturing most sarcastic content
  • Generalization: Strong performance across different news domains
